# Context
During the 2025 fall semester, I took English 1102, in which our objective was to write a research paper. In developing that paper, one of my optional tasks was to reach out to my college’s librarian. I spoke to Christine Kickels about my current development from my research to my potential ideas. She was impressed with my work and suggested I present it at the College of DuPage’s annual symposium.

My topic revolves around the YouTube algorithm and how it may shape the content viewers are most likely to see.

After conducting my research I boiled my thoughts into this statement:

> YouTube’s business model is designed to keep users engaged on the platform, but its recommendation algorithm can also sway viewers into increasingly segmented or narrow content.

# Result
By the end of a semester, I produced an 11-page research paper organized into sections: introduction, background, social and psychological impacts, improvements and solutions, and conclusion.
I prepared the following materials for the symposium; while many of these items were not considered for the award, I felt it was important to give the best presentation possible:
-	Bibliography
-	Self-reflection of developing research paper
-	PowerPoint
-	Field work in the form of a survey

Thankfully, the College of DuPage is hosting all of my presentation materials. These documents can be found at this link: [https://dc.cod.edu/srs/2026/schedule/27/](https://dc.cod.edu/srs/2026/schedule/27/). 

My original research paper can be found by clicking the blue “Download” button. Other materials can be found under the "Additional Files" heading.

# Personal Sentiment
I'd like to preface this by saying I was extremely grateful for both Professor McGrath's and Christine Kickels' support throughout my writing process and the lead-up to the symposium. I truly appreciate the time and effort they invested in helping me develop my work and prepare for the event.

I also applied for a research award and, unfortunately, was not selected. While I was disappointed, I remain proud of the research I conducted and the final paper I produced. The process challenged me to think critically, refine my writing, and explore a topic that I found genuinely meaningful.

One takeaway from the experience is that I gained a greater appreciation for the role presentation skills play in communicating research. Even the most insightful ideas can only make an impact if they are communicated in a way that engages an audience and encourages discussion.

After presenting, the floor was opened for questions. Having attended several presentations that evening, I was encouraged by the level of engagement from the audience during my session. Many attendees seemed to connect with the topic of YouTube's recommendation algorithm and reflected on their own viewing habits. Those conversations were one of the most rewarding parts of the experience and reinforced the value of sharing research with others.

Finally, one memory from the evening that I don't think I'll ever live down: my mother and girlfriend decided to attend the symposium to support me. Unfortunately for my mother, several hours of academic presentations proved to be a greater challenge than expected. As the evening went on, I caught her fighting off sleep during a few presentations.
